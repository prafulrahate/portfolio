Bike Sales Analysis using Excel

Summary of Bike Sales Analysis:

The analysis of bike sales is based on several pivot tables that provide insights into different aspects of the sales data. The tables include information on income, distance traveled, age groups, and customer age.

Income Analysis:

The first pivot table focuses on the average income of customers and their purchasing behavior. It reveals that customers with an income of "No" (indicating low income) have an average bike sales value of $26,500, while those with an income of "Yes" (indicating high income) have an average bike sales value of $33,333. The overall grand total average sales value stands at $28,621.

Distance Travelled Analysis:

The second pivot table examines the count of purchased bikes based on the distance travelled. It shows that customers who travel 0-1 miles and 1-2 miles have an equal count of 12 bikes purchased. For the 2-5 miles distance range, there is a count of 1 bike purchased. The 5-10 miles range has 8 bikes purchased, and the 10+ miles range has 2 bikes purchased. The grand total of bikes purchased is 46.

Group Analysis:

The third pivot table focuses on the count of purchased bikes by age groups. It shows that the adolescent age group has 8 bikes purchased, the middle age group has 22 bikes purchased, and the old age group has 2 bikes purchased. The grand total count of purchased bikes across all age groups is 46.

Customer Age Analysis:

The final pivot table displays the count of purchased bikes by customer age. It provides a breakdown of the count for different age values. For example, customers at the age of 25 have 1 bike purchased, customers at the age of 26 have 4 bikes purchased, and customers at the age of 27 have 2 bikes purchased. The table continues to provide the count for various age values, and the grand total count of purchased bikes is 46.

Overall, these pivot tables provide valuable insights into bike sales based on income, distance travelled, age groups, and customer age. The analysis reveals patterns and trends in customer behaviour, allowing for informed decision-making in marketing, product development, and customer targeting strategies.
